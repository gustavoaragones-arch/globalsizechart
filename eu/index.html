EU Size Conversion Tools

European sizing differs structurally from both US and UK systems because it is derived from the Paris point measurement method. One Paris point equals two-thirds of a centimeter, meaning EU footwear sizes are directly linked to foot length rather than a purely numeric baseline. This creates more standardized increments across continental brands.

Unlike US and UK sizing, EU footwear typically does not differentiate between men's and women's numbering systems. Instead, the same numeric scale applies to both, with fit differences determined by last shape and width rather than separate size charts. This unified system simplifies some conversions but introduces confusion when mapping to gender-specific US or UK charts.

Clothing sizing in the EU commonly uses numeric measurements based on body dimensions in centimeters. For example, men's suits and jackets often correspond directly to chest circumference in centimeters. Women's apparel also reflects proportional measurement standards rather than arbitrary numbering.

Because EU sizes are measurement-based, they often convert more accurately when foot length or body dimensions are known. However, brand-specific interpretation still exists, particularly among fashion-forward labels and athletic footwear manufacturers.

Our EU converters default to European sizing and allow accurate conversion to US, UK, Japan, and centimeter-based equivalents. This ensures consistency when purchasing from American or British retailers while maintaining alignment with continental standards.

For highest accuracy, measure foot length in centimeters before converting. EU sizing is closest to measurement logic, but cross-system rounding differences can still create half-size discrepancies.

EU Size Conversion FAQs

Why are EU shoe sizes different?

EU sizes are based on the Paris point system, which ties directly to foot length in centimeters.

Are EU shoe sizes unisex?

Yes. EU sizing typically uses the same numeric scale for men and women.

How do EU sizes convert to US sizes?

EU sizes convert using standardized comparison tables, but rounding differences can cause half-size variation.

Do EU clothing sizes use centimeters?

Yes. Many EU garments are sized according to body measurements in centimeters.

Is EU sizing more accurate?

EU sizing is closer to measurement logic, but brand differences still affect final fit.
