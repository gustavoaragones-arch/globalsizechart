How to Use Our Size Converter

How to Measure Shoes (CM-based)

▼

To get the most accurate shoe size conversion:

  1. Place your foot on a piece of paper against a wall
  2. Mark the longest point of your foot (usually the big toe)
  3. Measure from the wall to the mark in centimeters
  4. Use this measurement in our CM converter for the most accurate size

Tip: Measure both feet and use the larger measurement. Measure in the evening when your feet are at their largest.

How to Measure Clothing

▼

For Tops:

  • Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your chest, under your arms
  • Waist: Measure around your natural waistline

For Pants:

  • Waist: Measure around your natural waistline
  • Hips: Measure around the fullest part of your hips
  • Inseam: Measure from crotch to ankle (for length)

For Dresses:

  • Measure chest, waist, and hips as described above
  • Use the largest measurement to determine your size

Regional Differences

▼

US Sizing: Uses numeric sizes for shoes and letter sizes (XS-XXXL) or numeric waist sizes for clothing.

UK Sizing: Shoe sizes are typically 1 size smaller than US. Clothing uses UK-specific numeric sizes.

EU Sizing: Uses metric-based sizing. Shoe sizes are typically 1-1.5 sizes larger than US.

Asian Sizing (JP/CN): Generally runs smaller than US/EU sizes. Japanese and Chinese sizes use similar systems but may vary by brand.

Important: These conversions are approximate. Always check brand-specific size charts when available, as sizing can vary significantly between manufacturers.

Brand Sizing Disclaimer

▼

Size conversions provided on this site are based on standard international sizing conventions. However, sizing can vary significantly between brands and manufacturers. We recommend:

  • Always checking the brand's specific size chart when available
  • Reading customer reviews for fit information
  • When in doubt, ordering multiple sizes and returning what doesn't fit
  • Considering the type of fit you prefer (slim, regular, oversized)

This tool is designed to provide a starting point for size conversion, but individual fit preferences and brand variations should always be considered.
