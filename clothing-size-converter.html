How to Measure for Clothing

Measuring for Tops

▼

Chest Measurement:

  1. Stand straight with arms at your sides
  2. Wrap the measuring tape around the fullest part of your chest, under your arms
  3. Keep the tape level and snug but not tight
  4. Record the measurement in centimeters

Waist Measurement:

  1. Find your natural waistline (usually the narrowest part of your torso)
  2. Wrap the measuring tape around your waist
  3. Keep it level and comfortable
  4. Record the measurement

Measuring for Pants

▼

Waist Measurement:

  1. Measure around your natural waistline where you typically wear pants
  2. Keep the tape level and comfortable
  3. Record the measurement in centimeters

Hips Measurement:

  1. Stand with feet together
  2. Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your hips
  3. Keep the tape level
  4. Record the measurement

Inseam (for length):

  1. Measure from the crotch seam to the bottom of the ankle
  2. Or measure a pair of pants that fit you well from crotch to hem

Measuring for Dresses

▼

For dresses, you'll need three measurements:

  1. Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your bust
  2. Waist: Measure around your natural waistline
  3. Hips: Measure around the fullest part of your hips

Use the largest of these three measurements to determine your dress size. If you're between sizes, consider the style of the dress (fitted vs. loose) and order accordingly.

Fit Guide

Slim Fit vs Regular Fit vs Oversized

▼

Slim Fit: Closer to the body, typically 2-4cm smaller in key measurements. Choose your exact size or consider sizing up if you prefer more room.

Regular Fit: Standard fit with comfortable room. Use your measurements as-is for this fit.

Oversized Fit: Loose, relaxed fit. You may want to size down if you prefer a less baggy look, or stay true to size for maximum comfort.

Pro Tip: Always check the brand's fit description and customer reviews for specific fit information.
