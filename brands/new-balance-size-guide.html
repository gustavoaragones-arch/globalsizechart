New Balance Size Guide

New Balance size guide: brand sizing differences, EU vs US discrepancies, fit type (narrow/wide), and real-world fit tips. Convert New Balance sizes with our tools. Use the sections below to understand how New Balance sizes compare to standard charts, EU vs US differences, fit width, and real-world fit tips. For exact conversions, use our converters linked at the bottom.

Brand sizing differences

New Balance uses its own fit models and lasts; sizes often differ from generic conversion charts. Shoes may run small or large depending on the line.

EU vs US discrepancies

EU and US sizing don't align 1:1. New Balance products sold in Europe may be labeled in EU sizes; when buying from the US site you'll see US sizes. Use our converters below to translate between regions.

Fit type (narrow / wide)

New Balance is known for wide width availability (2E, 4E). Standard width runs true to size for many.

Fit Characteristics

Some users report that this brand may feel true to size or slightly roomy in the forefoot; fit can vary by model. If you experience tightness on the sides despite correct length, consider checking if wide (2E) or extra-wide versions are available for that style.

Real-world fit tips

Measure yourself and compare to New Balance's official size chart for the specific item. When between sizes, many buyers size up for comfort. Read recent customer reviews for fit notes.
